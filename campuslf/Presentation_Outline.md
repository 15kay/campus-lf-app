### **Slide 2-3: Problem Context & Community Need (3 minutes)**

#### **The Problem:**
- Students lose valuable items daily on campus
- No centralized system for reporting/finding lost items
- Current methods (notice boards) are ineffective
- Financial and emotional impact on students

#### **Community Impact:**
- 5,000+ students and staff on WSU campus
- Estimated 200+ items lost monthly
- Average item value: R500-R2000
- Current recovery rate: <10%

#### **Why Mobile?**
- Immediate access from anywhere on campus
- GPS location services
- Camera integration for photos
- Push notifications for real-time alerts
- Always available in users' pockets

- **Output:** Item recovery facilitation, community engagement, analytics

#### **Success Metrics:**
- Increase recovery rate to 60%+

### **Potential Questions & Answers:**

**Q: How does this differ from existing lost & found solutions?**
A: Our app is specifically designed for campus environments with WSU integration, real-time messaging, and mobile-first approach with GPS and camera features.

**Q: What about privacy and security concerns?**
A: We implement comprehensive input validation, WSU email verification, and users control what information they share. All data is sanitized and securely stored.

**Q: How would you scale this to other universities?**
A: The modular architecture allows easy customization of branding, email domains, and campus locations. The core functionality remains the same.

**Q: What was the biggest technical challenge?**
A: Implementing real-time messaging while maintaining offline capability and ensuring cross-platform compatibility were the main challenges.

**Q: How do you measure success?**
A: Success metrics include user adoption rate, item recovery percentage, user engagement (karma system), and community feedback.
